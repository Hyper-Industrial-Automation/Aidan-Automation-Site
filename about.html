About
Hyper Industrial Automation in plain terms.

Hyper Industrial Automation exists for a specific gap: manufacturers that need real automation and controls help, but not a full-scale integration firm.

Work is focused on small, well-defined projects: adding sensors, updating PLC logic, building simple control panels, wiring stack lights, or configuring basic vision jobs. In other words, the kind of work that removes friction from day-to-day production.

The person behind Hyper Industrial Automation is an automation engineer with hands-on experience in automotive manufacturing, presses, and general production environments. The emphasis is always on:

  • Clean, clearly labeled wiring
  • Readable, documented PLC logic
  • Simple, maintainable designs
  • Clear scoping of what is and isn't included

Safety circuits, guarding changes, and full robot systems are intentionally out of scope. That keeps engagements focused, straightforward, and aligned with practical plant-floor needs.
