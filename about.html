About
Process engineering background, focused on practical automation.

I'm a process engineer working in automotive manufacturing with hands-on experience in presses, sensors, small PLC projects, and practical automation changes that actually have to survive on a plant floor.

This freelance work exists for one type of client: teams that don't have a full-time controls engineer, but still need somebody who can speak the language of sensors, PLCs, and production.

I focus on small, well-bounded projects rather than chasing giant installations. That usually means:

  • Adding or fixing a sensor and tying it into PLC logic
  • Implementing a small reject or indication feature
  • Helping debug odd controls behavior in an existing cell
  • Documenting changes so maintenance isn't blind later

Safety systems, guarding, and high-risk modifications are intentionally out of scope. That keeps work tight, predictable, and easier to maintain.
